Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Like other Italian classics, eating a bowl of silky, rich, perfectly salty carbonara is life-changing. The classic Roman dish is composed of silky eggs, Sardinian sheep's milk cheese, and al dente Spaghett or spaghettoni is comfort food wrapped around your fork tines. The best part? It's completely possible to make it right at home. Read on for how to make yours the best it can be.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz pancetta
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 large egg
  • 4 oz finely grated Pecorino Romano
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. In a medium skillet over medium heat, cook pancetta, stirring occasionally, until golden brown and crisp, 20-25 minutes. Transfer pancetta to a paper towel-lined plate.
  2. Meanwhile, in a large pot, combine salt and 10 cups water and bring to a boil. Cook spaghetti, stirring occasionally, until al dente, 8-10 minutes; reserve 1/2 cup pasta water.
  3. While pasta cooks, in a large bowl, whisk egg yolks, egg, and cheese until just combined.
  4. Immediately transfer spaghetti to egg mixture. Add pancetta and 1/4 cup pasta water and stir, adding 2-4 tablespoons more pasta water if sauce seems too thick, until cheese is melted and sauce is smooth; season with pepper.
